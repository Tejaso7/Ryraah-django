How to understand if your Bracelet is Pure Silver?

Stunning silver steals the light like nothing else. Pure silver also possesses a great value in the market, so valuables prepared from the material tend to come with a levitated price tag than those that barely appear like silver. And so, like other valuable substances, silver also possesses unique properties that can assist you to recognize it.


Without making any noticeable mistake; almost everything these days in this world can be faked easily. So, if you're wondering if your piece of silver jewellery is real or fake or planning to buy a pure silver bracelet or a piece, here are a few ways to know if your bracelet and other items are created from pure silver or not.

 

  • A commercially brought-in silver object in almost all countries is engraved with one or more silver hallmarks depicting the purity of the silver, the tag of the manufacturer or silversmith, and other markings to depict the manufacturing date and other information about the silver piece.

  • Get some magnets and check if they get attracted to your silver piece or not. Silver is not remarkably magnetic and reveals only fragile magnetic effects, unlike iron, nickel, and cobalt metals. If your magnet attracts strongly to the piece, it possesses a ferromagnetic core and is not purely silver.

  •  Pure silver is completely odourless. If the piece smells like sulfur or posses a distinct metallic smell, then it is not a pure silver item. It could be a silver-plated item as well.

  •  Silver requires to be polished on a regular basis. Rust or a deficiency of oxidation depicts that the piece is prepared of a different element from silver. Rub and wipe the tarnished item as if polishing. If no black residue is seen, it's not pure silver.

  • Some pieces may be silver-plated, meaning that they are surrounded by a layer of original silver. Try to scratch the piece with your fingernail and check for another substance below it (try only if you have purchased the piece). If seen, then the item is plated with silver but is not completely a pure silver item.

  •  Pure silver items possess the capability to melt the ice at a rapid rate even at room temperature. So, if an ice cube melts faster when in contact with the silver-looking piece, it definitely pure silver.

 

So, these are all the key points you need to keep in mind to check if your bracelet is made up of pure silver or not.
